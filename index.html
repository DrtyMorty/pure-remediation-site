Fire & smoke restoration

The fire is out. The damage isn't.

Soot is acidic — it keeps etching metal, glass, and finishes for days after the flames stop. Smoke odor works into ducts, wall cavities, and soft goods far from where the fire burned. And the water that put the fire out starts the same clock as any flood. Fire recovery is three losses at once, and the order of operations decides what can be saved.

It's also usually the largest insurance claim a homeowner ever files. We photograph and inventory the damage to structure and contents from the first visit, build the line-item scope your adjuster will ask for, and coordinate with your carrier — the same claims-first approach we bring to every water loss.

Straight answers

The questions homeowners ask at 2 a.m.

What to do first, what it costs, and how insurance really works — no runaround.

On average about 45 minutes anywhere in Houston and Harris County, 24 hours a day. A real person answers the phone — not a call center — and a crew starts moving before you hang up.

Sudden, accidental water damage — a burst pipe, a failed water heater, a storm intrusion — is covered by most homeowner policies. Gradual leaks and outside flooding can be treated differently. We document the loss the way adjusters expect and can walk you through your coverage before you file.

Most homes dry in three to five days, depending on how much water there was, which materials it reached, and how long it sat. We place industrial equipment the same day and take moisture readings daily until the structure reads dry — we don't pull equipment early.

Mold can start growing within 24 to 48 hours of water intrusion. Small areas are usually safe to stay near while we contain and remove them; larger growth may call for temporary relocation. We contain the area first to stop spores from spreading, then verify with air-quality testing before sign-off.

It depends on the type of damage, the size of the affected area, and how long it has been there. Our free estimate tool gives an honest range in about a minute, and we put a written quote in your hands after on-site instrument readings — no surprise costs.

Yes. We work with claims from all major carriers, prepare adjuster-ready documentation — photos, moisture logs, and line-item scope — and coordinate directly with your carrier so you're not stuck playing middleman.

Fire is one of the most consistently covered perils in homeowner policies, and that coverage usually extends to smoke and soot damage and to the water used to put the fire out. A fire claim is often the largest claim a homeowner ever files, so documentation matters: we photograph and inventory the damage to structure and contents and prepare the line-item scope your adjuster will ask for.

Smoke doesn't stay where the fire was. It travels through ductwork and wall cavities and settles as acidic soot on surfaces far from the source, where it keeps corroding metal, glass, and finishes until it's removed. That's why even a small kitchen fire calls for a room-by-room assessment. Our fire and smoke work follows IICRC S700, the industry standard for fire and smoke damage restoration.

Removed — if it's done in the right order. Masking odor with fragrance fails within weeks. Following the IICRC S700 approach, we remove the charred and soot-laden materials that are the odor's source, clean the remaining surfaces, then treat the air and soft goods. If firefighting water soaked the structure, we dry that too, so a smoke problem doesn't become a mold problem.
